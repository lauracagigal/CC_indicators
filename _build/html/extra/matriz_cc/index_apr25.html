Atmosphere 1. Atmospheric Concentration of CO2 1.1.1 Linear Trend
Download
Figure 1 Monthly Mean Concentration of Atmospheric CO2 at Mauna Loa since 1959. The blue line represents the monthly mean values, centered in the middle of each month. The red line represents the same, after correction for the average seasonal cycle. The solid black line represents the trend, which is statistically significant (p < 0.05). The annual oscillations at Mauna Loa are due to the seasonal imbalance between the photosynthesis and respiration of plants on land. From NOAA ESRL Global Monitoring Division. https://www.esrl.noaa.gov/gmd/ccgg/trends/

1.1.1 Amount of Hot Days and Cold Nights
Download
Figure 4 Annual percent of hot days and cold nights relative to 1961–1990 climatology at Koror. Hot days are defined as days above the 90th percentile for that same calendar day (e.g., January 15th) from the 1960–1990 period, while cold nights are defined as days below the 10th percentile for that same calendar day in the 1960–1990 period. The solid black lines represent statistically significant trends (p < 0.05).

Figure 3 Trends in annual dry days (top) and maximum number of consecutive days (bottom) over the period 1951–2024 at Koror. Dry days are defined as days below 1mm (0.04 inches) threshold. Consecutive dry days is a measure of the longest sequence of days in a year where rainfall is less than 1 mm (0.04 inches).
